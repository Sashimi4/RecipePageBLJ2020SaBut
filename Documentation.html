Marble Cake

This week i have decided to choose this marble cake as a pefect beginner's bake.

Recipe:

  • 250 g Butter
  • 250 g Sugar
  • 1 Pack Vanilla-sugar
  • 4 Eggs
  • 8 Tablespoons of Milk
  • 300 g White Flour
  • 2 Teaspoons of Baking Powder
  • A Pinch of Salt
  • 150 g Salt
  • a little bit of Powered Sugar
  • 4 Tablespoons of Chocolate Cream

Step 1

To start things of, I would lay out all necessary ingredients forthis Cake. In Step 1 we are gonna use a little bit of that butter to grease up the baking pan. We dont want it to stick at the end.


Melt down the butterand mix it with the sugar and vanilla-sugar. Keep mixing until the sugar is fully disolved. It just start to look like a more liquid custard.

Step 2

After everything has disolved into a liquid mass, We will need to add the four eggs.


In the image I'am splitting the eggyolk from the eggwhite. But this isn't that important, since it all comes together at the end. But it can make the cake more fluffy, so I would recommend it.

Step 3

Mix it well all together and add inbetween the milk.

Here again more mixing.

What a great plate.

Step 4

With our yellow goop we are onna add the whiteflower.

First we mix the flour with the pinch of salt and baking powder. Afterwards we add it.


I would recommend here a sieve of some type. It will make sure that there are no flour chunks, which will in return make a nice fluffy cake.

Step 5

A Glorious Mountain. So Beautiful. But here we can see how the sieve has done it's magic.

Step 6

Now comes the chocolate mixture. I used a Ovomaltine based mixture. I used Ovomaltine-spread and -powder, thats why I used a bit more milk to activate the powder. First we have to half the original mixture. Afterwards we can add the chocolate to the other-half. So now we have a light mixture and a dark one.

Step 7

Make sure the form has been greased.

If it all checks out, add one of the mixtures first and then the other on top. I personally added the dark mixture first.


To give it that marbled effect, use a fork and just run the both mixtures with it. That is what will create the famous marbled texture.

Step 8

We are almost at the finish line.

Now you have to preheat the oven at 210 degrees Celcius or 410 Fahrenheit or for those scientists out there 483.15 Kelvin.

Then once it's reached the heat, pop it in the oven for 60 min.

Step 9

You should, be able to see it rise. A nice crust should also be forming on the outside. After the 60 min, poke a hole in the cake with a toothpick or something of the sort. If there is no material on the toothpick the cake is Ready.


Let it cool down. Then add the powdered sugar to give it that christmas feeling.
